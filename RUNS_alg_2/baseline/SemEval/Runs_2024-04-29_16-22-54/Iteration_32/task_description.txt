CTRs serve as exhaustive sources, supplying intricate information on clinical trial protocols, results, and safety and efficacy details. The fundamental aspects of CTRs comprise:

1. Patient Criteria: Clearly stated inclusion and exclusion standards
2. Procedure Specifications: Thorough intervention descriptions, including dosage, administration method, and duration
3. Assessment Information: Comprehensive patient demographics, diagnostic procedures, and trial results
4. Adverse Effects: Documented instances of adverse reactions and related symptoms.

Expert clinicians can scrutinize individual CTRs or a group of reports for comprehensive evaluation.
