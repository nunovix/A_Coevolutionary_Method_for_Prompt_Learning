Analyze the semantic connections between Clinical Trial Report (CTR) elements and clinical domain experts' statements. CTRs represent the trial design and results for assessing new treatment efficacy and safety. Each study consists of one to two patient groups, named cohorts or arms, characterized by unique treatment approaches or patient characteristics. A CTR comprises the following components:

1. Patient Selection: defining patient acceptance/exclusion requirements.
2. Therapeutic Intervention: outlining treatment protocol, including dosage, frequency, and duration.
3. Outcome Assessment: reporting trial participant number, measurement data, units, and outcomes.
4. Safety Analysis: documenting recorded adverse events and patient responses.

Domain experts' statements are factual declarations conveying details about a specific CTR or comparisons among multiple CTRs.

Evaluate semantic congruence between clinical domain expert declarations and Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs). Ensure consistency between statements and CTR contents, preventing contradictions. CTRs consist of four main sections: (1) Eligibility Criteria defining patient selection, (2) Intervention detailing treatment type, dosage, schedule, and duration, (3) Results reporting statistical analysis, outcome measures, units, and conclusions, and (4) Adverse Events recording observed symptoms and signs. Clinical statements make assertions about the information in these CTR sections, either regarding a single CTR or comparatively between two CTRs.
