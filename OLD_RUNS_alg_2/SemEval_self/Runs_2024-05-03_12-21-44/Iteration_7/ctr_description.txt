The descriptions below are drawn from a standard part of Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs).
----------
1->

1. Background and Objectives, Experimental Design and Methods: outlines the study background, objectives, and methods used, including participant characteristics, intervention description, and primary and secondary endpoints with the corresponding statistical analysis methods. 

2. Results and Discussion: presents the findings of the study and the interpretation of the results. 

3. Conclusion: summarizes the main implications of the study and its contributions to the field. 

4. References: lists the sources of information and literature cited in the study.
----------
2->

The Experimental Design and Methods section in Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs) outlines the study design, including the methods used for participant selection, data collection, and statistical analysis. The Participant Characteristics section specifies the demographic and clinical features of the trial participants. The Intervention section describes the experimental treatment or procedure being tested. The Primary and Secondary Endpoints section outlines the trial's primary and secondary outcomes and the statistical analysis methods used to evaluate them.
----------
3->

CTRs consist of the following components:

1. Experimental Design and Methods: outlines the study design and methods used.
2. Participant Characteristics: specifies demographic and clinical features of trial participants.
3. Intervention: describes the experimental treatment or procedure being tested.
4. Primary and Secondary Endpoints: outlines the trial's primary and secondary outcomes, along with the statistical analysis methods used to evaluate them.
----------
4->
A Clinical Trial Report (CTR) is structured into six main sections: Background and Objectives, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion, and References.
