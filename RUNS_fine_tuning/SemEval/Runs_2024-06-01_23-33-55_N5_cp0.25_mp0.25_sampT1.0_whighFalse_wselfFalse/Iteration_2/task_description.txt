1->Your task is determining support relationships between individual sections of Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs) and clinical statements. CTRs detail the methodology and findings of clinical trials, assessing effectiveness and safety of new treatments. CTRs consist of 4 sections: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA listing conditions for patient participation, (2) INTERVENTION description specifying type, dosage, frequency, and duration of treatments, (3) RESULTS summary detailing participants, outcome measures, units, and conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS listing signs and symptoms observed. Statements make claims about information in these sections, either for a single CTR or comparing two.

Each trial involves 1-2 groups of patients subjected to different treatments or baseline characteristics. CTRs are segmented into four distinct parts: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA, specifying inclusion and exclusion conditions for patient enrollment, (2) INTERVENTION, describing the therapeutic approach, including treatment type, dosage, administration frequency, and treatment length, (3) RESULTS, detailing the number of participants, outcome metrics, units of measurement, and trial conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS, noting any adverse signs and symptoms experienced by the patients. Clinical experts make claims based on these sections for a single CTR or in comparative analyses between two. Confirm entailment by verifying that the expert's claim pertains to the content of the CTR, is substantiated by the CTR, and aligns with the given descriptions.
