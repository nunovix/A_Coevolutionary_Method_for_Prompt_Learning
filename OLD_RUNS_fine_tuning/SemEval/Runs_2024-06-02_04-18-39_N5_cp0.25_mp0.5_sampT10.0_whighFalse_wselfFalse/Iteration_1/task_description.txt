1->Evaluate semantic entailment connections between sections of Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs) and expert clinical statements. CTRs document the methodology and results of clinical trials aimed at determining the effectiveness and safety of new treatments, involving 1-2 patient cohorts with varying treatments or baseline characteristics. Comprehensive CTRs include four sections: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA detailing inclusion conditions for trial participants, (2) INTERVENTION outlining treatment specifics such as type, dosage, frequency, and duration, (3) RESULTS summarizing participant counts, outcome measures, units, and conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS listing observed patient signs and symptoms. Statements made by clinical experts claim information within these sections, either for a single CTR or in comparative analyses between two CTRs. To establish entailment, ensure the statement is supported by the CTR, does not contradict provided descriptions, and accurately reflects the trial information.
