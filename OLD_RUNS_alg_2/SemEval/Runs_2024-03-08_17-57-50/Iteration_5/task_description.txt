CTRs provide detailed information on clinical trials, evaluating the efficacy and safety of novel treatments. Each trial includes cohorts or arms with unique treatments and varying baseline characteristics. Comprehensive CTRs consist of four main sections: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA, outlining patient inclusion conditions, (2) INTERVENTION details, specifying treatment type, dosage, frequency, and duration, (3) RESULTS summaries, presenting participant statistics, outcome measures, units, and conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS cataloging observed signs and symptoms. To establish entailment, the statement's claim must align with clinical trial data, be supported by the CTR, and not contradict the provided descriptions.
