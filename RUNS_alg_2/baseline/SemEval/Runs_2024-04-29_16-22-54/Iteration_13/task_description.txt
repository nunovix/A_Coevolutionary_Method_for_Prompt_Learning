Evaluate the alignment of clinical experts' statements with corresponding sections in Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs). Expert statements must agree with CTR content and avoid contradiction. CTRs serve as comprehensive records of clinical studies, detailing trial methods, outcomes, and treatment efficacy and safety. A comprehensive CTR includes:

1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA: patient selection guidelines
2. INTERVENTION: treatment methodology, dosage, frequency, and duration
3. RESULTS: participant demographics, assessment tools, and study summaries
4. ADVERSE EVENTS: documented adverse effects and symptoms

Experts may reference specific CTRs or compare multiple reports.
