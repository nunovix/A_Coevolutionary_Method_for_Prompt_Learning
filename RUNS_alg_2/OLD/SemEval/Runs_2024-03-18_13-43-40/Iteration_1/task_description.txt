CTRs serve to outline the methodologies and findings of clinical trials, focusing on the efficacy and safety of new treatments. The four sections include:

1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA: Specifies patient inclusion conditions.
2. INTERVENTION: Details treatment type, dosage, frequency, and duration.
3. RESULTS: Summarizes participant statistics, outcome measures, units, and conclusions.
4. ADVERSE EVENTS: Lists observed signs and symptoms.

Statements under consideration propose claims about the CTR sections, either pertaining to a single report or in a comparative analysis of two reports.
