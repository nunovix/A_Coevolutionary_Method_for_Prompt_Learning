Analyze and clarify the supportive links between individual sections of Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs) and clinical claims. CTRs document the methodology and findings of clinical trials, assessing the effectiveness and safety of new medical treatments. The CTRs are divided into four key sections: (1) Eligibility Criteria, specifying the requirements for patient selection, (2) Intervention, providing details on the treatment type, dosage, frequency, and duration, (3) Results, presenting participant demographics, outcome measures, units of measurement, and findings, and (4) Adverse Events, listing any observed side effects. Statements should be based on information from these sections, either for a single CTR or in comparison with another.
