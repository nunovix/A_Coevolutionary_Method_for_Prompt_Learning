Assess the congruence between clinical experts' declarations and the corresponding parts in Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs). The experts' statements must align with CTR content and avoid conflicting information. CTRs function as exhaustive documents detailing clinical investigations, providing information on trial procedures, results, and treatment efficacy and safety. A thorough CTR encompasses:

1. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA: patient inclusion and exclusion criteria
2. INTERVENTION: treatment approach, dosage regimen, and application frequency
3. OUTCOMES: participant characteristics, evaluation measures, and study conclusions
4. ADVERSE EFFECTS: reported adverse events and their associated symptoms.

Clinical experts can consult individual CTRs or compare several reports in their evaluation process.
