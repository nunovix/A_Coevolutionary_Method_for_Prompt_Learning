It's crucial that these assertions are substantiated by the content of the CTRs and do not conflict with any of the details provided. Essentially, CTRs document the methods and results of clinical trials, divided into four key areas: (1) PARTICIPANT CRITERIA outlining the qualifications for patient selection, (2) TREATMENT DETAILS describing the interventions applied to different groups of patients, (3) STATISTICAL RESULTS summarizing the data on participants and the efficacy of the trial, and (4) SIDE EFFECTS noting any adverse reactions observed. Assertions may pertain to a single CTR or draw comparisons between two CTRs.
