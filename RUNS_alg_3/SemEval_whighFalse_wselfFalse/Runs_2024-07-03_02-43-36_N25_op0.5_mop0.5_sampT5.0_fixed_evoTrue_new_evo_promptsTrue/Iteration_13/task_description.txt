CTRs detail the methodology and outcomes of clinical trials aimed at assessing new treatments' effectiveness and safety. These reports are divided into four key sections: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA, outlining the conditions for patient inclusion, (2) INTERVENTION, describing the treatments administered to different patient cohorts, (3) RESULTS, summarizing participant statistics and trial outcomes, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS, listing observed signs and symptoms during the trial. Expert statements should make claims about the information within these sections, either for a single CTR or when comparing two CTRs. To establish an entailment relationship, the claim must be directly related to the clinical trial information, supported by the CTR, and must not contradict the provided descriptions.
