CTRs encapsulate the protocols and results of clinical trials, evaluating the efficacy and safety of novel therapeutic interventions. Break down CTRs into four key components: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA, detailing inclusion and exclusion criteria for trial participants, (2) INTERVENTION, outlining the specific type, dosage, frequency, and duration of treatments administered, (3) RESULTS, summarizing participant demographics, outcome measures, units of measurement, and trial conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS, documenting any observed signs and symptoms. Analyze and substantiate clinical claims based on these components, either referencing a single CTR or drawing comparisons between two.
