Maintain agreement between clinical experts' claims and corresponding sections in Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs). Reconcile professionals' declarations with CTR data, eliminating inconsistencies. CTRs serve as extensive resources, supplying details on clinical study protocols, results, and safety and efficacy data. Key components of CTRs include:

1. Patient Criteria: Clearly stated inclusion standards
2. Procedure: Detailed intervention description, including dosage, administration method, and duration
3. Assessment: Demographic information, diagnostic techniques, and trial results
4. Adverse Reactions: Documented incidents of adverse events and related symptoms.

Clinical experts can examine single CTRs or several reports to ensure comprehensive evaluation.
