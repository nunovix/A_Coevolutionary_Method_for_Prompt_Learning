The descriptions below are drawn from a standard part of Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs).
----------
1->
A Clinical Trial Report (CTR) comprises the study objective, design and methodology, executed procedures and interventions, trial results and their interpretations, and the conclusive findings summary. These elements are standard components of a CTR.
----------
2->
CTRs include the following descriptive sections:

1. Study Design: Outlines the experimental design and methods used in the clinical trial.
2. Participants: Provides demographic and baseline characteristic information of the study population.
3. Interventions: Describes the experimental intervention(s) and control group(s).
4. Outcomes: Reports the primary and secondary outcome measures and their corresponding results.
5. Statistical Analysis: Describes the statistical methods used to analyze the data.
6. Results: Summarizes the findings of the trial in a tabular or graphical format.
7. Discussion: Interprets the results and provides a clinical context for their significance.
8. Conclusion: Summarizes the main findings of the trial and their implications.
----------
3->

A Clinical Trial Report (CTR) includes the following six sections:

1. Background and Objectives (rationale and aims of the trial)
2. Methods (design and interventions employed)
3. Results (data presentation and findings)
4. Discussion (interpretation and analysis)
5. Conclusion (summary and implications)
6. References (cited sources)
