Confirm the conformity of clinical experts' statements with the designated sections in Clinical Trial Reports (CTRs). Maintain agreement between professionals' statements and CTR data to prevent contradictory details. CTRs are comprehensive documents detailing clinical investigations, including trial procedures, outcomes, and safety and efficacy information. Key components of a thorough CTR are:

1. Patient Eligibility Criteria: selection guidelines
2. Intervention Procedures: methodology, dosage, frequency, and duration
3. Assessment Metrics: patient demographics, diagnostic techniques, and trial results
4. Adverse Events: recorded side effects and symptoms.

Clinical experts can review individual CTRs or compare multiple reports for comprehensive evaluation.
