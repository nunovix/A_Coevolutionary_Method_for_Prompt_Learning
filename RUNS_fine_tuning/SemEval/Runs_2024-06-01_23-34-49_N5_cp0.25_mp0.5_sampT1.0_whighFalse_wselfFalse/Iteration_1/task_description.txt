CTRs encapsulate the procedures and results of clinical trials, evaluating the efficacy and safety of novel therapeutic interventions. Break down CTRs into the following four components: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA, which outlines the prerequisites for patient inclusion, (2) INTERVENTION, detailing the nature, dosage, administration frequency, and treatment duration, (3) RESULTS, summarizing participant demographics, outcome metrics, units of measurement, and findings, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS, documenting observed signs and symptoms. Analyze and substantiate clinical claims based on these components, either in relation to a single CTR or when contrasting two.
