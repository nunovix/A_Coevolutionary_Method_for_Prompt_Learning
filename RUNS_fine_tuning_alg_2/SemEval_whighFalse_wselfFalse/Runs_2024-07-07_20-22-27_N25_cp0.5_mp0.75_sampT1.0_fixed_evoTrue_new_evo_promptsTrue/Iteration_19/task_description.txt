CTRs encapsulate trial protocols and findings for assessing new therapies' effectiveness and safety. Patients are grouped into cohorts or arms, each subjected to different treatments or with distinct baseline characteristics. A thorough CTR includes four essential components: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA for patient selection, (2) INTERVENTION specifics such as treatment type, dosage, frequency, and duration, (3) RESULTS detailing the number of participants, outcome metrics, measurement units, and conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS noting any patient symptoms experienced during the trial. Expert commentary must align with the CTR content, be evidence-based, and remain consistent with the reported data.
