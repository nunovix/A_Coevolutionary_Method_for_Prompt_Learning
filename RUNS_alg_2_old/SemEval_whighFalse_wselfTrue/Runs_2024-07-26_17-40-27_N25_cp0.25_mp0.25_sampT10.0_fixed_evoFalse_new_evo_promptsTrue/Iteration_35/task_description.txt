These reports categorize participants into different cohorts or arms, each receiving varied treatments or having unique baseline characteristics. A thorough Clinical Trial Report is systematically organized into four principal sections: (1) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA, which specify the qualifications for patient participation, (2) INTERVENTION, detailing the treatment specifics such as type, dosage, frequency, and duration, (3) RESULTS, providing a summary of participant numbers, outcome measures, units of measurement, and conclusions, and (4) ADVERSE EVENTS, recording any adverse symptoms observed during the trial.
