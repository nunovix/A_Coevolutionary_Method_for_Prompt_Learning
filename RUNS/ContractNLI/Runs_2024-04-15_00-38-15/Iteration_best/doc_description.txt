Under a valid Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), parties commit to protecting designated confidential data, as defined in the agreement. The agreement specifies the duration and scope of confidentiality obligations, which apply solely to identified confidential information and involved parties. Permissible disclosures include consensual and legally mandated disclosures, as outlined in the agreement. Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information may lead to penalties, such as fines, litigation, and other remedies for breaches. Upon request or agreement termination, parties are obligated to return or destroy all Confidential Information. The NDA falls under the specified jurisdiction and law, with confidentiality provisions excluding Sections 4, 5, and 8. Confidentiality exceptions are outlined in the agreement, specifically consensual disclosures.
