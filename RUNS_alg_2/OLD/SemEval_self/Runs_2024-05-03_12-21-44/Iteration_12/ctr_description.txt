A Clinical Trial Report (CTR) is structured into six key components:

1. Background and Objectives: Establishing the trial's rationale and intended outcomes
2. Methods: Designing and executing interventions
3. Results: Presenting and analyzing data
4. Discussion: Interpreting and evaluating findings
5. Conclusion: Summarizing implications and takeaways
6. References: Citing relevant sources

Clinical trial reports (CTRs) consist of the following segments:

1. Study Design: Details the experimental design and techniques employed in the clinical investigation.
2. Participants: Offers demographic and baseline data regarding the trial subjects.
3. Interventions: Describes the experimental treatment(s) and control group(s).
4. Outcomes: Reports the primary and secondary outcome indicators and their respective findings.
5. Statistical Analysis: Describes the statistical techniques applied to analyze the data.
6. Results: Presents the trial results in a tabular or graphical manner.
7. Discussion: Interprets the results and provides a clinical interpretation of their importance.
8. Conclusion: Summarizes the key findings of the trial and their implications.
----------

CTRs include the following descriptive sections:

1. Study Design: Outlines the overall plan and structure of the trial.
2. Methods: Details the procedures used in conducting the trial.
3. Statistical Analysis: Describes the methods used to analyze the data obtained from the trial.
4. Results: Presents the findings from the trial.
5. Discussion: Interprets the results and relates them to the existing literature.
6. Conclusion: Summarizes the main findings and implications of the trial.
7. References: Cites the sources of information used in the trial.
